## Instructor Tips

### Why Use This Mode?
- **Builds pressure management**: Simulates ED/acute care pace
- **Consequence awareness**: Students see "what could happen"
- **Priority training**: All options are wrong - which is safest?
- **Clinical reasoning**: Time pressure reveals true understanding

### When to Assign
‚úÖ **Good for**:
- After covering infection control (Ch 18-19)
- Before clinical rotations
- High-acuity/critical care prep
- NCLEX Next Gen practice

‚ùå **Not ideal for**:
- First-time content exposure
- Students struggling with basic concepts
- Study mode review (use regular chapters)

### Discussion Points After Play
1. "Which decision surprised you?" (Check consequences)
2. "Why did you hesitate?" (Look at >45s questions)
3. "What would you do differently?" (Learn from -10s)

## Common Student Questions

**Q: "Why am I losing points for correct answers?"**  
A: If you took >45 seconds, you get +3 instead of +10. In real emergencies, delayed correct decisions can still harm patients.

**Q: "Can I replay questions?"**  
A: Yes! Anti-memorization allows 3 attempts per 24 hours. Use it to improve response time.

**Q: "What if I run out of time?"**  
A: 0 points. This teaches assessment speed - critical for high-acuity environments.

**Q: "Why are all answers wrong?"**  
A: To teach prioritization. Real nursing = choosing least dangerous option when perfect isn't possible.
