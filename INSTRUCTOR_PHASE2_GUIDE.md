## Instructor Tips

### Before Play
**Set Expectations**:
- "You have THREE critical resources"
- "Wrong answers create MORE questions"
- "Once used, resources are GONE"
- "This simulates real resource constraints"

### During Play (if watching)
Watch for:
- **Panic when resources deplete** (teachable moment about scarcity)
- **Strategic hesitation** (good! they're thinking)
- **Frustration at escalations** (real consequence awareness)

### After Play - Discussion

#### 1. Resource Usage
**Ask**: "Did anyone finish with all 3 resources intact?"
- **If yes**: Excellent! No mistakes triggered escalations
- **If no**: "Which resource did you use? Was it worth it?"

#### 2. Escalation Experience
**Ask**: "How many got escalation questions?"
- Count: Who got 0, 1, 2, 3, or 4?
- Discuss: "Which crisis was hardest to manage?"

#### 3. Resource Regret
**Ask**: "Did anyone wish they'd saved a resource for later?"
- Connect to real nursing: "Only 1 ICU bed, 3 critical patients"
- Or: "One rapid response team, two codes happening"

#### 4. The "No Perfect Answer" Realization
**Ask**: "What happened when you needed a resource but it was gone?"
- This is the point! Sometimes nurses have NO good options
- All choices are bad - pick least bad

## Common Student Reactions

### "This is unfair! I ran out of resources!"
**Response**: "Exactly. Real nurses work with limited resources. What did you learn about prioritization?"

### "I got 9 questions, they got 5!"
**Response**: "Because your decisions had consequences. Just like real patient care - errors compound."

### "I didn't know it would trigger more questions!"
**Response**: "Real mistakes don't come with warnings either. That's why FIRST decision is critical."

## Quick Troubleshooting

### "Students are panicking about escalations"
**Solution**: Frame it as learning, not punishment. "Mistakes help you learn - that's why we practice!"

### "They want to replay to avoid escalations"
**Great!** That's the point - learning from mistakes. Allow replays.

### "Someone triggered all 4 escalations and gave up"
**Intervention**: "This is EXACTLY when nurses can't give up. What did you learn about staying focused under pressure?"
